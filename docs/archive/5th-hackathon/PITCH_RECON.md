## Format we should expect

- **~16 finalists**, 1-hour pitch window + jury voting → roughly **3 minutes per team** (matches Hack-Nation's prior 4th edition format).
- Judges include **Greylock VCs** plus partner reps from sponsors (Google, OpenAI, DeepMind, SAP, Meta, Amazon, AWS, MIT Sloan, **Databricks** for our challenge).
- Awards ceremony 3:45 PM ET after pitches.
- Q&A may or may not happen — assume it does, prepare for 1-2 questions per team.

## Top 5 patterns to imitate

1. **Hook in the first 10 seconds** — single arresting statistic OR a 5-second clip of the product working. People remember opens and closes; the middle is fog.
2. **Power of Three** — "There are many things I could tell you, but here are the three to remember." Forces simplification, signals discipline.
3. **Plain English over jargon** — "We deliver groceries to homes" beats "next-generation AI-resolver of grocery needs." Translation: lead with "an ASHA worker types a patient situation and gets a tier-ranked answer in 30 seconds" — not "a 12-tool agentic supervisor coordinating heterogeneous data planes."
4. **Healthcare evidence layer** — investors expect clinical/operational credibility. Show numbers tied to reality: 10,000 facilities (real dataset), Trust Score 65/100 with CI (auditable), Validator WARN flag (self-checking), 0 errors / 12 queries (audited).
5. **Demo is the hero** — devote ~1 minute to either live demo or a tight pre-recorded clip. Judges remember the product working more than they remember slide bullets.

## Top 3 anti-patterns to avoid

1. **"Powered by AI" hand-waving** — name the model, name the eval, name the moat. Healthcare investors specifically reject this.
2. **Spending 60+ seconds on the problem before revealing the solution** — judges already understand healthcare matters. Get to the product fast.
3. **Generic boxes-and-arrows architecture diagram** — use the bespoke `/architecture` page screenshot OR skip the slide entirely. Stock diagrams scream "template."

## Healthcare-specific deck obligations (from RunwayTeam 2026 guide)

Healthcare decks are not SaaS decks. Investors will ask about:

- **Where the training data comes from** → Virtue Foundation 10k facilities (open dataset, cited).
- **Explainability** → Trust Score with 7 contradiction rules + cited evidence; Validator agent with PASS/WARN/FAIL.
- **Validated performance** → 12-query eval, 100% robustness gap closed, P95 49.5s.
- **Durable moat** → on-device PHI extraction + healthcare-architect domain depth + Databricks-native (judges grade on this).
- **Regulatory pathway** → name HIPAA + ABDM/ABHA + FHIR R4. Show this is not an afterthought.
- **Buyer / payer logic** → ASHA workers + NGO planners (operating budget) → state-mission policy makers (capacity planning) → eventually hospital-VPC enterprise (Databricks Model Serving).

## What kills hackathon pitches

- Overrunning time. Three minutes means **2:45 spoken**, leaving breath room.
- Live demo failing live (rehearse the fallback, have the 60s video queued in browser).
- Reading slides verbatim — talk *around* slides, not from them.
- Apology language ("I know we didn't have time to..." — DO NOT). Project authority.
