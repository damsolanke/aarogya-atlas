# REHEARSAL — 90 minutes, four runs, then stop

**Total: 90 min user time. Run 1→4 in order. Do not skip.** The deck is fixed. The only thing left to tune is your delivery.

Open these before you start:
- `http://localhost:8888/backup.html` (press `f` for fullscreen, `s` for speaker)
- `docs/pitch/SCRIPTS.md` (the 3-min finalist script)
- `docs/pitch/QA_PREP.md` (20 questions × 30s)
- A timer (phone stopwatch is fine — keep it visible, not in your hand)
- A glass of water

---

## Run 1 — Cold read with timer (15 min)

**Goal:** find out where the script breaks against your real voice. Do not fix anything.

1. Stand up. Speak at presentation volume — not mumbled, not shouting.
2. Start timer. Press `→` to advance from slide 1.
3. Read the 3-min script aloud while advancing slides. Resist the urge to stop and fix mid-sentence.
4. When you hit the closer ("From symptom, to care."), STOP the timer.
5. Capture in a notepad or REHEARSAL_NOTES.md:
   - **Final time** (target: 2:45–2:55)
   - **3 stumble points** — exact words where you tripped, paused, or mispronounced
   - **2 places you ran over** — slides where the script was longer than the visual
   - **1 thing that landed** — a sentence that felt right; keep it untouched

Do NOT re-run yet. One cold read is enough data.

---

## Run 2 — Tighten and re-time (20 min)

**Goal:** edit the script based on Run 1, hit 2:45.

1. Open `SCRIPTS.md`. For each stumble point from Run 1, do ONE of:
   - **Replace tongue-twister with simpler phrasing** (e.g. "agentic" → "agent-driven", "asyncio fan-out" → "twelve tools at once")
   - **Cut the sentence entirely** if it doesn't carry weight
   - **Split into two short sentences** if it's a comma-laden run-on
2. Cut filler everywhere: `basically`, `actually`, `essentially`, `kind of`, `sort of`, `you know`, `obviously`, `at the end of the day`. None of them earn their seconds.
3. For each over-run slide, identify ONE phrase to drop. Don't trim across the whole script — make targeted cuts.
4. Read it aloud once more, full speed, with timer.
5. Target: **2:45 ±5s**. If you're at 2:55+, cut another 15 words. If under 2:30, you're rushing — slow the demo beat (slide 4) and the closer.

---

## Run 3 — Q&A roulette (30 min)

**Goal:** 25-second answers, not 30. Judges interrupt at ~25–30s.

1. Open `QA_PREP.md`. Number the 20 questions 1–20.
2. Round 1: pick 5 random numbers. Say each answer aloud, timed.
3. **If any answer >25s, rewrite shorter** in the margin of QA_PREP.md. Cut backstory, cut hedges, lead with the punchline.
4. Round 2: pick 5 different numbers. Repeat. Aim ≤25s.
5. Round 3: pick the 5 hardest from your gut — the adversarial / curveball ones. These are where you lose.
6. Total: 15 questions answered aloud. If any answer felt slow, weak, or rambling, mark it and rewrite tonight even if it's not in your random 15.

**Five rules during this run:**
1. **Repeat the question** in your first sentence. Buys 2 seconds, signals you heard, lets other judges catch up.
2. **Lead with the answer.** "Yes — and here's why" beats "So the way to think about that is..."
3. **One number per answer.** Generic answers lose. "Mosaic VS endpoint, 10,000 rows, 22% latency win" beats "we use Databricks effectively."
4. **Bridge back to a moat in the last sentence.** Multimodal, on-device PHI, or the equity counterfactual.
5. **"I don't know"** is acceptable, paired with "what I do know is...". Never bullshit.

If a surprise question surfaces during this run that isn't in QA_PREP.md, write the question + a 25s answer at the bottom of QA_PREP.md.

---

## Run 4 — Full dress with QuickTime recording (25 min)

**Goal:** you, on camera, doing the full pitch + simulated Q&A, watching it back once.

1. Open QuickTime Player → File → New Movie Recording. Frame yourself the way Zoom will see you.
2. Hit record.
3. Open the deck, press `f` for fullscreen, press `s` for speaker (move speaker window to a second display or hide it offscreen).
4. **Pitch:** start the timer, deliver the full 3-min pitch top to closer.
5. **Q&A simulation:** still recording, ask yourself 5 questions from QA_PREP.md aloud, then answer each in ≤25s.
6. Stop recording. Watch it back ONCE at 1.25× speed.
7. Note **3 fixes only** — don't fix more than 3:
   - One filler / verbal tic to kill
   - One slide where the script and the visual are out of sync
   - One Q&A answer that needs to be shorter or clearer
8. Implement the 3 fixes in `SCRIPTS.md` / `QA_PREP.md` — **do NOT re-record**. The point of a dress run is data, not perfection.

---

## After Run 4 — STOP

You're done with rehearsal. Move to `docs/pitch/PRECALL.md`. Resist the urge to keep tuning. Over-rehearsal makes you robotic; under-rehearsal makes you ramble. Four runs is the sweet spot.

**Two lines to repeat before you close this file:**
1. *"In rural India, a postal code can decide a lifespan."* (the open)
2. *"From symptom, to care."* (the close)

If you can say those two lines crisply, on cue, at presentation volume, with eye contact at the camera — the rest is execution.
